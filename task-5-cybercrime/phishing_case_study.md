## Case Study: Phishing Attack on a First-Year Student

A first-year student recently experienced a phishing attack while checking emails related to internships.  

While scrolling through his inbox, he came across an email that seemed **too good to be true**. Curious and hopeful, he began investigating it further.  

During this time, he received a phone call from someone claiming to be a staff member of the college. The caller insisted that he should apply for the same opportunity mentioned in the email, making the situation seem more legitimate and urgent.  

Trusting the information, the student opened the link provided. However, within a few minutes, he received a shocking message — **₹50,000 had been deducted from his bank account.**  

Panicked, he immediately approached the actual college staff. After a proper enquiry, it became clear that he had fallen victim to a scam.  

Taking quick action, he reported the incident to the **cybercrime helpline** and informed his bank about the fraudulent transaction. After several days of back-and-forth communication and verification, he was finally able to recover his money.  

---

### Key Takeaway  
Even a moment of trust in the wrong place can lead to serious consequences. Always verify before clicking links or sharing sensitive information — especially when it involves money or urgency.
